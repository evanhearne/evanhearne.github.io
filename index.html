1. A Rationale for the Approach to the Brief

1.1 Research

Given the situation we are in, the WHO's message is clear: In order for us to control this virus, there are a few principles needed. They are social distancing, facial protection, and sanitation. In my project I will be focusing on social distancing. Social distancing has been proven to be very effective, with 2 metres being the most optimum. The respiration droplets expelled doesn't surpass 1.75 metres generally, so 2 metres is the most optimum to avoid these droplets containg COVID-19.

Summary: I will work on devices that allow users to know how far away they are from other people, provided they both have one of these devices.

Key Questions:

  • How will the device know the proximity between itself and another device?
  • How will the user know that they are safe, or not safe?

1.2 Response to the Brief

Social distancing has proven difficult to maintain in larger areas such as schools, supermarkets and public buildings. It has been shown that supermarkets are a very high source for coronavirus cases, and not a lot more has been done recently to tackle these cases. Equally, schools around the country are being closed every week due to cases popping up. It is hard for people to guess how far two metres is in some situations, like when there are shelves and other objects scattered around buildings. If people knew how far two metres was, they could help protect themselves from the virus. My project will entail a solution to this, by giving people a device that allows the wearers to know if they have breached the 2 metre distance by flashing a light. This should help in the reduction of covid cases as people are actively making an effort to restrain the virus.

Summary:My project will allow people to maintain social distancing by using a device that emits light when two metre proximity has been lessened.

Key Questions:

  • How will the device work if there is an object blocking both people carrying the device?
  • How does the device differentiate between different types of devices (Microwaves, TV, FM Radio), given that the device works by radio signal?
